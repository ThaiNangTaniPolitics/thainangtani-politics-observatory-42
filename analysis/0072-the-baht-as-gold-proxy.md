## 0072 – The Baht as Gold Proxy: A Currency That Rises While the Economy Falls, and Who Pays for It
### *How a metal-linked capital flow became Thailand's exchange rate, what it costs the people who produce, and why the central bank reaches for the periphery*

-----

## 1. Scope and the contradiction

Between 2025 and mid-2026 the Thai baht appreciated sharply while every real-economy indicator moved the other way. A currency that strengthens as output weakens is not a sign of health; it is a signal that the exchange rate has decoupled from production and is being set by something else.

This node establishes what that something else is, who bears the cost, and how the policy response fits the pattern documented in [0069](0069-the-way-out-runs-through-the-politics.md) (K-shaped concentration) and [0066](0066-the-dual-system.md) (the dual system).

**The divergence, in numbers:**

| Indicator | Direction |
|---|---|
| Baht vs USD | **+9% in 2025**, a further ~1.5% into 2026; approached **31/USD**, a four-year high |
| GDP growth | **2.5% (2024) → 1.8% (2025) → 1.7% (2026 forecast)** |
| Competitiveness | Bank of Thailand (Jan 2026 report): **in decline** |
| Exports | Hit by US tariffs (**19%**, 40% on transshipped goods) and margin compression |
| Tourism | Falling arrivals, notably Chinese; price-uncompetitive against Vietnam |
| Household debt | ~90% of GDP (BoT) / **104% including informal** (Chula/JSCCIB) |

**2026 forecast range:** 30.80–33.00 baht/USD.

-----

## 2. The mechanism: the baht as a gold derivative

Thailand is a major regional gold trading hub, and gold is the traditional savings instrument of Thai households. The transmission runs: **world gold price rises → Thai holders sell gold for dollars → dollars converted into baht → baht appreciates.** The flow is financial, not productive; it reflects the metal's price, not Thai output.

**The evidence:**
- Correlation between the world gold price and the baht in 2025: **0.80–0.88 — the highest among emerging Asian currencies.**
- Gold-linked FX flows reached, at times, **as much as 20% of total market activity.**
- Khaosod English (20 March 2026) frames it directly: the baht now functions as a **gold proxy**, traded internationally as a metal bet rather than as a claim on the Thai economy.
- Contributing factors also cited: a weakening US dollar, expectations of Fed rate cuts, seasonal Q4 inflows, and a large current account surplus.

**Correlation is not causation — state it as correlation.** The precise weight of gold versus dollar weakness is contested (see §3). What is not contested is that the exchange rate is being driven by capital flows rather than by trade competitiveness.

**On the current account surplus:** a surplus during collapsing domestic demand is not evidence of export strength. It partly reflects **suppressed imports** — households that can no longer afford to buy. Read alongside 104% household debt, the surplus is a symptom of weakness presenting as a strength.

-----

## 3. The official denial, contradicted by the official response

The Bank of Thailand's senior director of reserve management stated: **"The strengthening of the baht is not due to gold, but rather to the weakening of the dollar and a large current account surplus."**

Yet the BoT's own actions point the other way:
- **December 2025:** the central bank issued an urgent warning to the government on the need to act on the baht's value.
- **Tightened scrutiny** of dollar transactions and of gold traders' FX inflows.
- **From March 2026: a cap on gold transactions of 50 million baht (~US$1.62 million) per day**, explicitly to curb the appreciation.

**Analytical point:** a central bank does not impose capital controls on a channel it considers immaterial. The denial and the intervention cannot both be right. For external use, the defensible formulation is: *the BoT publicly downplays the gold channel while regulating it.*

-----

## 4. Who pays and who gains — the distributive core

An overvalued currency is not distributionally neutral. It is a transfer.

**Bears the cost:**
- **Exporters** — margins compressed; industry groups flag export declines of **0.5%–1.5%**.
- **Agricultural exports specifically** — identified as hardest hit precisely because they rest on domestic production and domestic labour. That means farmers.
- **Tourism** — Thailand priced out relative to Vietnam in a price-elastic market; the sector is ~60% of GDP's service base.
- In short: the tradable, labour-intensive, provincial economy.

**Gains:**
- Importers; holders of foreign-currency debt; anyone whose wealth is spent or held abroad.
- Gold traders intermediating the flow itself.

**This is the K-shape in monetary form.** BoT Governor **Vitai Ratanakorn** has stated on record that resources are "concentrated in the hands of large businesses" and described a K-shaped recovery in which large corporates access finance while low-income households and SMEs do not ([0069](0069-the-way-out-runs-through-the-politics.md)). The exchange rate now performs the same sorting: it rewards financial position and penalises production.

-----

## 5. The pattern of the instruments: periphery, not structure

Two BoT interventions in the same period, both aimed at the edges of the system:

| Measure | Target | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Cash deposits >5m baht require proof of origin; enhanced due diligence on withdrawals >5m (−35% observed) | the informal / grey **cash** economy | from Q4 2026 |
| Gold transactions capped at 50m baht/day | **retail and trader gold** flows | from March 2026 |

Both are legitimate instruments in themselves. But note what neither touches: the concentration the Governor himself diagnosed. Corporate and elite wealth does not sit in cash deposits or gold tickets — it sits in equity, real estate, offshore structures and nominees. The tools reach the grey cash economy and the small saver; the named structural problem remains unaddressed. **Same mismatch as the 200bn "energy transition" tranche ([0069](0069-the-way-out-runs-through-the-politics.md) Postscript): the instrument does not fit the stated diagnosis.**

-----

## 6. Data discipline (before any external citation)

- **Growth forecasts diverge and both are January 2026:** World Bank / EPRS say **1.7%**; Bank of Thailand says **2.3%**. Always name the source. Do not present one as "the" figure.
- **Household debt:** BoT ~90% of GDP; Chula/JSCCIB **104% including informal debt**. Different definitions, not a contradiction — say which.
- **Gold's causal weight is contested** (§3). Use "correlation of 0.80–0.88" and "the BoT regulates the channel it downplays"; do not assert "the baht rose because of gold".
- **The 20% figure** (gold-linked share of market activity) is "at times", not a steady state.
- **US tariffs:** 19% on exports, 40% on transshipped goods.

-----

## 7. The thesis

Thailand's exchange rate has stopped being a price set by what the country produces and has become a price set by what the world pays for gold and by what the Federal Reserve does to the dollar. The result is a currency strong enough to hurt every sector that employs people, in an economy growing under 2%, with household debt above 100% of GDP.

The state's response is to regulate the small end of the flow while leaving untouched the concentration its own central bank governor has named. That is not a monetary accident. It is the dual system ([0066](0066-the-dual-system.md)) expressed in the exchange rate: **financial position appreciates, productive labour depreciates.**

## Discipline checklist (verification record)

- [x] **Correlation stated as correlation.** The gold–baht relationship is given as a **coefficient (0.80–0.88, 2025)** and as a share of market activity ("**at times** as much as 20%"), never as "the baht rose because of gold." The competing explanations — dollar weakness, Fed-cut expectations, seasonal Q4 inflows, current account surplus — are carried in the text, not suppressed.
- [x] **The official denial is quoted, not overridden.** The BoT's senior director of reserve management is quoted verbatim denying the gold channel. The node does not call this false; it sets the denial beside the intervention (scrutiny of gold traders' FX inflows; the 50m baht/day cap from March 2026) and draws the defensible inference: **a central bank does not impose controls on a channel it considers immaterial.** External use: "the BoT publicly downplays the gold channel while regulating it."
- [x] **Diverging figures named with their sources.** Growth 2026: **1.7% (World Bank / EPRS)** vs **2.3% (Bank of Thailand)**, both January 2026 — never present one as "the" figure. Household debt: **~90% (BoT)** vs **104% incl. informal (Chula/JSCCIB)** — a definitional difference, stated as such.
- [x] **Distribution as analysis, not as intent.** The node claims that an overvalued currency **functions** as a transfer from tradable, labour-intensive sectors to holders of financial position. It does **not** claim that anyone engineered the exchange rate to harm farmers. Export-decline figures (0.5%–1.5%) are attributed to industry groups.
- [x] **The surplus reading is marked as reading.** "A current account surplus during collapsing domestic demand partly reflects suppressed imports" is an economic interpretation, offered as such and anchored to the 104% household-debt figure — not presented as an official finding.
- [x] **Instrument–diagnosis mismatch, not accusation.** Both BoT measures (cash >5m baht; gold cap) are acknowledged as legitimate in themselves. The point is coverage: neither reaches equity, real estate, offshore structures or nominees — where the concentration the Governor himself named actually sits. Same structure as the 200bn "energy transition" mismatch in [0069](0069-the-way-out-runs-through-the-politics.md). No allegation of bad faith.
- [x] **Novelty calibrated.** The gold-proxy dynamic is reported (Khaosod, Nation, Bangkok Post) and the harm to exporters and tourism is covered (The Diplomat, ICIS, MUFG). The node's contribution is the **synthesis**: reading the exchange rate as a distributive mechanism continuous with the K-shape Vitai described, and placing the two peripheral capital controls beside the untouched structural concentration.
