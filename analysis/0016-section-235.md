**1. THE MECHANISM (Section 235)**
Section 235 of the 2017 Constitution establishes the primary legal track for removing elected officials without criminal conviction. It empowers the National Anti-Corruption Commission (NACC) to bypass criminal courts for "serious violations of ethical standards."

**2. PROCEDURAL FLOW (The Pipeline)**

  * **Trigger:** An allegation of a "serious ethical breach" (often linked to Section 112 reform initiatives).
  * **Inquiry:** NACC conducts a fact-finding mission. If a majority (not less than half) finds grounds, the case splits.
  * **The Supreme Court Track:** Under Section 235 (1), cases of ethical non-compliance are referred directly to the Supreme Court for a ruling.

**3. CONSTITUTIONAL SANCTIONS**
Unlike criminal law, Section 235 provides for:

  * **Immediate Suspension:** Once the Court accepts the case, the MP must cease duties unless ordered otherwise.
  * **Lifetime Bans:** Conviction results in permanent loss of the right to stand for any election (Section 235, para 4).
  * **Loss of Voting Rights:** A ban of up to 10 years on the right to vote.

**4. STRUCTURAL FINDING**
Section 235 creates a **Parallel Judiciary**. It allows the state to achieve the terminal exclusion of political actors (Lifetime Bans) using a lower evidentiary threshold ("Ethics") than required for criminal treason or sedition.

# 0017 – The Jurisprudence of "Prevention": A Philosophical Audit

### From Defensive Democracy to Interpretive Interventionism

[Home](https://www.google.com/search?q=/thainangtani-politics-observatory/) | [Analysis](https://www.google.com/search?q=/thainangtani-politics-observatory/analysis/) | [Archive](https://www.google.com/search?q=/thainangtani-politics-observatory/archive/)

**1. PHILOSOPHICAL ANCHOR (Section 49)**
The legal "logic" of the 2024–2026 interventions rests on a specific reading of **Section 49**: "No person shall exercise rights or liberties to overthrow the democratic regime with the King as Head of State."

**2. DOCTRINAL SHIFT: THE "PRE-EMPTIVE STRIKE"**
Traditional constitutionalism requires a "clear and present danger" for intervention. Thai jurisprudence has shifted to a **Pre-emptive Model**:

  * **Legislative Intent as Threat:** The mere act of *proposing* an amendment is treated as a "preparation" to undermine the order.
  * **The Logic of "Capability":** Rulings (e.g., 3/2567) focus on whether an act is *capable* of causing harm in the future, rather than whether harm occurred.

**3. ETHICS AS "OBJECTIVE MORALITY"**
The philosophy treats "Ethics" (Section 160, 219) not as a personal code, but as a **Mandatory Constitutional State**.

  * **Subjective to Objective:** Judges transform subjective moral values into objective legal requirements.
  * **Result:** A "serious violation of ethics" is no longer a matter of corruption, but a failure to align with the institutional "truth" defined by the Court.

**4. CONCLUSION: THE FRAGILE MANDATE**
Philosophically, this elevates **Institutional Oversight** above **Sovereign Will**. The "Democratic Mandate" (Section 3) is no longer absolute; it is a *conditional grant* that remains valid only as long as it satisfies the non-material ethical criteria of the non-elected branches.
