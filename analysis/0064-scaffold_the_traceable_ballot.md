## 0064 - SCAFFOLD: The Traceable Ballot
**The Traceable Ballot: Vote Secrecy, the 2026 Barcode System, and the Prosecution of Those Who Proved the Breach**

### THESIS (load-bearing — keep as the spine)
The 8 February 2026 general election placed **unique, individually traceable codes** on ballots, linking each ballot through a numbered counterfoil to the voter. The breach of secrecy is **structural** — it occurs at the moment of voting, not the count — and is **not cured** by the Election Commission's "stored separately" defence. The same architecture that built the traceability **prosecutes, for sedition, the citizens who decoded it to prove it** — making the breach simultaneously real and, by design, unprovable from outside.

-----

## I. What changed (the documentary core)
- 2024 and earlier: codes denoted the **booklet/batch**, identical throughout a booklet → NOT individually traceable.
- 2026: **unique per-ballot codes**, running number matching the **counterfoil serial** → individually traceable.
- Colour map: constituency (green) = QR; party-list (pink) = barcode; referendum (yellow) = none.

## II. The identification chain (confirmed)
barcode/QR → counterfoil serial → voter number → voter list → person.

## III. Stated rationale vs. real effect
- Authorised under **Clause 129, EC Regulations on the Election of MPs** — codes added "without prior notice", for anti-counterfeiting / distribution control (detect over-printing, verify 20-per-booklet, prevent "floating ballots", assign accountability if ballots leak).
- Rebuttal: **authentication ≠ identification.** Anti-forgery needs watermarks / microprint / special fibres, NOT unique per-ballot codes. Uniqueness serves identification.

## IV. The EC's shifting story (self-contradiction, double-sourced)
- 13 Feb: the barcode "identifies the booklet, **not** the voting choice."
- 18 Feb: the EC "acknowledged that the serial numbers and barcodes **could be used to trace a voter's ballot paper**."

## V. Why "stored separately" does not cure the breach (DRAFTED — the one formulated section)

The Election Commission's principal defence is that ballots and counterfoils are kept in separate storage, so the identification chain cannot be completed. This does not meet the constitutional standard. A secret ballot is guaranteed not by a custodian's promise to keep two records apart, but by the **impossibility of reconstructing the link between voter and vote**. Security that depends on trusting the record-holder is not a security property; it is a courtesy. The defence fails on four independent grounds, each sufficient on its own:

1. **Reversibility.** Separate storage can be recombined. A barrier that the party holding both halves is free to remove is not a guarantee — it is a postponement.

2. **The digitisation gap.** Physical separation is meaningless if the ballot–counterfoil correspondence was captured in digital form at any point *before* the records were separated — during printing, distribution control, the count, or the "authentication" process. The Commission has not disclosed whether, or at what stage, such a digital record was created. Its own stated rationale — detecting over-printing, verifying booklet completeness, assigning accountability if ballots leak — describes precisely the kind of tracking system that would generate one. The burden is on the Commission to show that no digital link exists; it has not discharged it.

3. **Mutability over time.** A storage arrangement maintained today guarantees nothing tomorrow. Records that exist can be linked later — under a different administration, for a different purpose. The secrecy of a vote cast in 2026 cannot depend on the future discretion of every future custodian.

4. **Absence of independent verification.** The separation is self-attested by the Commission — the same body whose conduct is under challenge — and is subject to no public or independent audit. Where the sole assurance is "trust the custodian," and where citizens who attempted to verify the codes have themselves been prosecuted, the claim is rendered unfalsifiable from the outside. An unfalsifiable assurance is not evidence of secrecy; it is its absence.

Taken together, these grounds move the secret ballot from a **design property**, which can be verified, to a **custodial promise**, which cannot. A constitutional guarantee of secret suffrage (Sections 83 and 85) cannot rest on the continued goodwill of the authority that designed the traceable system in the first place. The proper question is not whether the link has been exploited, but whether it *can* be — and on the Commission's own account, it can.

## VI. Irreparable by a clean count
The breach occurred at the moment of voting, not the count; a clean recount cannot repair it. The chilling effect — voters who voted differently for fear of the code — is unmeasurable, so the dataset's reliability cannot be restored.

## VII. The legal tracks
- **Constitutional Court:** Ombudsman petition, accepted **6–3 on 18 March 2026**, seeking to determine whether the 8 Feb election should be void; basis **Sections 83 and 85**; EC granted 15-day extension and submitted written clarification; **ruling pending**.
- **Administrative Court:** PP candidates Prasit Puttamapadungsak and Weerapat Kantha (data-leak concern).
- **Criminal (Section 157):** People's Party complaint against the EC and its secretary-general (Central Criminal Court for Corruption and Misconduct Cases).

## VIII. The self-defence loop
The EC moved against **six citizens** (sedition + membership of an unlawful secret society) for photographing and decoding the QR codes at the **22 February re-run election in Khan Na Yao, Bangkok**, to demonstrate the secrecy flaw. The proof is criminalised → the "not proven" evidentiary bar becomes **unmeetable by design**. (Parallel to the Section 112 self-defence loop in 0062: the architecture prosecutes its documenters.)

## IX. The administrator removed — accountability by spreadsheet (June 2026)

In June 2026 the EC **secretary-general, Sawaeng Boonmee** — the named defendant in the Section 157 complaint (VII) and the official who administered the barcode election and moved against the six decoders (VIII) — faced dismissal. The trigger was neither the barcode breach nor the Senate-collusion case in which he is co-accused, but a **performance score**: under **Section 56 of the organic law on the EC**, the secretary-general must reach 60% in the annual evaluation; he reportedly fell below it. Decision expected mid-June, under the new EC board chaired by **Narong Klunwarin**.

**The administrative constant.** Sawaeng has overseen every contested national vote of the era: the **2019 general election** (party-list seat-formula dispute), the **2023 general election** (the blocking of election-winner Pita Limjaroenrat), the **2024 Senate "self-selection"** (blue-senator collusion → 0061), and the **2026 barcode election** (this node). On the documentary record he is the single executor common to all four — multiply sourced (Bangkok Post, Nation, Thai Examiner).

**The procedural irony.** The evaluation is itself tainted: four commissioners who scored him — **Ittiporn Boonpracong** (chairman, stepped down 6 Dec 2025), **Santad Siriananphai, Lertviroj Kowattana, Pakorn Mahannop** — did so *after leaving office*, though the contract requires evaluators to be *serving* commissioners. The body that polices electoral procedure cannot run its own internal procedure cleanly. (Ex-commissioner **Somchai Srisutthiyakorn**: contradictory to remove Sawaeng for poor performance while the EC itself stays beyond scrutiny.)

**Why him, why now? — hypotheses, not established motive.** The four ex-commissioners accepted a *fresh* legal risk (a void evaluation) to lock in his removal; the size of the risk taken is itself the measure of the stakes. Candidate readings, each falsifiable:
- **Blame-allocation / self-protection** — the four and Sawaeng are *co-accused* in the Senate-collusion case; branding him a "poor performer" pre-positions the administrator as the operational fall guy ("commissioners set policy; the secretary-general botched execution"). *Tell:* timing tracks collusion-case milestones.
- **Discrediting a witness** — he knows the mechanics; a dismissed "failure" is a weaker accuser if he shifts blame upward. *Tell:* he later testifies / counter-accuses.
- **Acting before the power lapses** — done invalidly, in the window before the outgoing board lost standing and Narong's board could intervene. *Tell:* the new board reverses it.
- **Control of the machinery** — the secretary-general runs the apparatus that administers elections; the post is worth capturing. *Tell:* a faction-aligned successor is installed.
- **Null hypothesis (kept honest):** genuine poor performance (barcode, advance-voting anomalies), scored low and executed sloppily — but routine review does not require former officials to vote without authority; the *risk taken* is what the benign reading cannot explain.

**What it is not.** Not the accountability the contested elections demand. The substantive reckoning — the Section 157 complaint, the Constitutional Court petition, the Supreme Court collusion cases — is displaced by an internal metric. Sawaeng is **not an innocent victim** (he is co-accused in the substance); the point is that a **performance score is the wrong instrument for the right problem**, and it shields the institution: one official is offered up while the commissioners and the capture remain. The captured body's "self-cleaning" is a move *within* the impunity game, not an exit from it.

## X. The verdict as an open data point
Do NOT predict the outcome. Note the irony: the court reviewing the breach is itself one of the "independent agencies" whose capture the broader thesis alleges — the ruling *tests* whether any accountability layer functions. A "not proven" dismissal would itself be the evidentiary-bar manoeuvre (cf. Olarn, "no legal evidence").

## XI. Synthesis
Vote traceability = surveillance applied to the franchise. Olarn's "democracy as performance" made literal: the **form** (an election) preserved, the **function** (secrecy) hollowed.
